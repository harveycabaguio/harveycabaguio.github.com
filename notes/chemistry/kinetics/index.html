Kinetics & Rate of Reaction

Definitions

Reaction Rate

The change in concentration of a reactant or a product per unit of time, expressed in Mol/L*s. Rate can be altered by changing:

  • Surface area
  • Reactants
  • Temperature
  • Adding a catalyst

Collision Theory

Atoms, ions, molecules must collide with sufficient energy and in the correct orientation for a reaction to occur.

Activated Complex

A transition state where a temporary unstable arrangement of atoms breaks and forms new bonds.

Activation Energy

The minimum amount of energy required for a reaction to occur.

Catalyst

A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed in the reaction.

Reaction Mechanism

A series of steps that a complex reaction follows. It goes from reactants to intermediates, and intermediates to products.

Rate Determining Step

Slowest step in a reaction mechanism. It determines the rates of the overall reaction.

Intermediate

A substance that is both created and then consumed in a reaction mechanism. It does not get written in the overall equation.

Complex

A reaction with 2 or more steps.

Exothermic Reaction

A reaction that gives off heat.

Endothermic Reaction

A reaction that absorbs energy.

Concentration

The higher the concentration, the more molecules available to collide, therefore reaction rate tends to increase, as long as enough energy has been added.

Collision Theory and Reaction Rate

Collision theory says:

"In order for a reaction to occur the particles must collide. If particles do not collide, there is no reaction. Not all collisions cause chemical reactions. Particles must collide with sufficient kinetic energy and in the correct orientation."

This energy is called the activation energy (Ea).

Activation energy is defined as: the minimum amount of energy required for particles to effectively produce a chemical reaction.

Factors Affecting Reaction Rate

Temperature

As temperature increases, particles gain more energy. This means that they move faster, and they are more likely to collide.

More collisions, the greater the chance of an effective collision.

Surface Area

More surface area means more particles are exposed and have the opportunity to collide. This means you have an increased chance of successful collisions.

Concentrations

More concentration puts more molecules in to a certain space. More particles means more collisions.

Catalysts

Definition:

A chemical used to increase reaction rate, but it is not consumed in the reaction.

A catalyst weakens the intermolecular forces of reactant molecules.

  • This makes the bonds of reactants easier to break

    • This means less activation energy is required

      *Catalysts work by lowering activation energy
